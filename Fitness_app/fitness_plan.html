📏
10% Running Rule: Never increase weekly running distance by more than 10% vs the previous week. Brick runs follow this rule — e.g., if you ran 3 km last Saturday, max next Saturday is 3.3 km. Breaking this rule is the #1 cause of shin splints and stress fractures.
🔄
80/20 Training Rule: 80% of all sessions should be easy (Zone 2, conversational pace, RPE 4–5/10). Only 20% hard. This applies to cycling, running, and swimming. Going too hard too often is why most people plateau or get injured.

Collagen Timing — Most Commonly Done Wrong
Dr. Keith Baar research: collagen must be taken 30–60 min BEFORE exercise with Vitamin C to stimulate tendon and ligament collagen synthesis. Taking it after a workout provides NO tendon benefit. Mix 10–15g collagen in orange juice (Vit C source), then warm up 30 min, then train. This directly builds your ACL scar tissue, patellar tendon, and Achilles. Do this every training day.
