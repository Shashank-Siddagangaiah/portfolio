# AI Adoption Research — Enterprise & Data Engineering (2025)

Research compiled for manager presentation on AI tool usage in daily data engineering work.

## Key Statistics

- **78%** of companies deploying AI in at least one business function (McKinsey, 2025) — up from 55% in 2023
- **3.7×** average ROI on enterprise AI investments across industries (McKinsey, 2025)
- Text-to-SQL tools (QueryGPT, Pinterest text-to-SQL) in production at Uber, Pinterest, and Intuit
- AI fluency increasingly listed as a baseline requirement in data engineering job descriptions

## What Managers Care About Most

1. **Measurable business impact** — time saved, quality improvement, error reduction
2. **Practical implementation path** — phased roadmap, success criteria, resource requirements
3. **Risk transparency** — data security, governance framework, validation approach
4. **Comparative economics** — AI tool vs. hiring additional headcount

## Effective Presentation Structure for IT/Engineering Managers

- Lead with the **problem** being solved, not the technology
- Each slide title should be a **declarative statement** (not a topic label)
- Include a **governance slide** — IT managers always ask about data security and validation
- Show **real work examples**, not hypothetical use cases
- End with a **specific ask** — demo offer, license request, or team standard proposal

## Common Mistakes in AI Adoption Presentations

- Technology-first narrative instead of problem-first
- Generic ROI claims without organizational context
- Vague implementation plans ("we'll adopt gradually")
- Missing IT department concerns: security, integration, monitoring
- No acknowledgment of validation or error correction

## AI in Data Engineering — 2024/2025 Trends

- **Text-to-SQL** tools reducing manual query writing at enterprise scale
- **AI-assisted pipeline design** — from requirements to validated output
- **Data observability** with AI anomaly detection becoming standard
- **dbt Cloud AI** for model scaffolding and validation
- Data engineers shifting from boilerplate SQL to strategic optimization
